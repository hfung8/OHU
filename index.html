Ugandan Orphan Crisis

Uganda is home to approximately 2.4 million orphaned children.[1] Uganda’s orphan crisis has worsened through the decades. Children in Uganda are orphaned or abandoned most often due to illness or poverty in their families, frequently a combination of both. Additionally, many children may have parents who are living with HIV. The death of a father has been a major contributing factor for orphanhood due to gender inequality and the lack of women’s right.

As a child in Uganda, you are at risk of extreme poverty and have a high chance of contracting malaria and HIV, along with the looming danger of conflict. Children continue to be left to defend themselves against life’s harsh realities.
